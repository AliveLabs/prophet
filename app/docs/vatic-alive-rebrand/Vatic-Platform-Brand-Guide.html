Brand Strategy

Vatic is the competitive intelligence platform that gives you prescient, actionable insight into market movement. We sit between the world's signals and your decision-making, translating complexity into clarity.

Strategic Positioning

As a vertical-skinnable platform within Alive Labs, Vatic provides the foundational architecture for competitive intelligence across industries. Each vertical (Ticket, Neat, Forge) inherits the core platform and extends it with domain-specific signals, terminology, and visual identity.

Prescient

We predict before others see. Our signal processing delivers forward-looking intelligence that anticipates market movement.

Direct

No noise. No interpretation layer. Just the signal and what it means, delivered with precision and clarity.

Sharp

Competitive intelligence is a tool for decisive advantage. We're designed for strategists, operators, and decision-makers.

Logo System

The Vatic mark is a stylized V with an upward vertex, grounded in the Forge palette. The vertex dot marks the point of insight—where data becomes understanding.

Usage Rules

  • The mark always includes the vertex dot in Forge Gold (#D4880A)
  • Minimum size: 40px for digital applications, 10mm for print
  • Clear space: 1/4 of mark height on all sides
  • Never scale disproportionately or rotate
  • On light backgrounds: Carbon (#2B353F) arms, Lumen negative space
  • On dark backgrounds: Lumen (#F6F8FA) arms, Forge Black negative space

Color Roles

Primary (Carbon)

Structural color for headlines, primary buttons, focus states, and key UI elements. Paired with white for maximum contrast.

Accent (Forge Gold)

Highlights, key stats, vertex dot in logo, interactive elements that need attention. Warm and energetic.

Data Positive (Forge Patina)

Success states, positive signals, upward movement. Tinted backgrounds for success alerts.

Surface System

Forge Black (base), Forge (elevated), Forge Card (modals/cards), with Lumen and Warm Lumen for light surfaces.

Typography

Vatic uses three typeface families, each with a specific role in the information hierarchy and user experience.

Display Font: Space Grotesk

A geometric sans-serif used for headlines, cover text, and moments requiring bold visual presence. Weights: 300–700.

Vatic brings clarity to competitive noise.

UI Font: Inter

The primary typeface for body text, labels, and interface elements. Highly legible at small sizes with balanced letterforms. Weights: 300–700.

Inter scales perfectly from 12px captions to 18px body text. Its neutral character supports rapid scanning and deep reading.

Code Font: Space Mono

A monospace typeface for code blocks, technical documentation, and token names. Weights: 400, 700.

Brand Voice & Tone

Vatic's voice is prescient, direct, and sharp. We speak to strategists and operators who value clarity over comfort, insight over interpretation.

Prescient

We anticipate. Our language signals forward motion, pattern recognition, and the ability to see what others miss. "Market shift detected." "Competitor expanding into adjacent segment."

Direct

No layer of interpretation. We say what the signal means without hedging or softening. "This matters." "Act on this." Verbs before adjectives. Precision before politeness.

Sharp

Competitive advantage is earned. Our tone respects the user's intelligence and their need for speed. Short sentences. Active voice. Word economy.

Example Microcopy

Alert header: "Competitor pricing shift detected"

Not: "We noticed a competitor's pricing may have changed"

CTA: "View full competitive analysis"

Not: "Click here to see more information"

Empty state: "No signals yet. Expand your tracking scope."

Not: "There are no items to display at this time"

Error recovery: "Signal ingestion paused. Reconnect your data source."

Not: "Error: Unable to retrieve data"

Personality & Microcopy Patterns

Each interaction with Vatic should feel intelligent, fast, and purposeful. Microcopy guides without hand-holding.

Patterns by Context

Onboarding

"Set up your first signal." Action-oriented. We're starting somewhere.

vs. "Welcome to Vatic! Let's get you started."

Confirmation

"Tracking 12 competitors in Energy sector." Specific, concrete, shows what was done.

vs. "Successfully added!"

Guidance

"Add >5 competitors for stronger signals." Helpful constraint, not a scolding.

vs. "Please add more competitors."

Urgency

"Major shift in competitor Q2 roadmap." Signal strength is implied by specificity.

vs. "URGENT: Something important!"

Usage Don'ts

Protect the brand by avoiding these mistakes:

Don't mix palettes

The old Vatic indigo (#5B4FFF) and teal (#0D9488) are deprecated. Use Carbon and Forge Patina instead.

Don't override base tokens

If you're implementing a skin, override :root and .dark values only. Don't change component structure or animation definitions.

Don't use Lava (#FF4500)

Lava is reserved for Alive Labs corporate/display contexts. Use Ember (#FF7849) for warm accent needs in Vatic.

Don't reduce contrast below WCAG AA

All foreground-on-background combinations must meet 4.5:1 minimum. Test with color contrast checkers before shipping.

Don't skip dark mode testing

The .dark override block must be tested with every UI change. Dark mode is not optional.

Don't add unsanctioned colors

If you need a new color, propose it in the Alive Labs Design System first. Colors are intentional.
