01 · Brand Strategy
Compressed Intelligence

The ticket is the most information-dense document in a restaurant. Every modification, every fire time, every table position — compressed into a slip of thermal paper that a line cook reads in under two seconds. Ticket (the product) compresses competitive intelligence the same way: everything happening in your market, delivered in the time it takes to read a ticket.

Everything in this identity traces back to this story. The palette is thermal paper warmth — clarity under pressure. The type system is compression without waste. The mark is the physical ticket. The voice is the expeditor — the person at the pass who compresses chaos into calls.

Positioning Statement

Ticket is competitive intelligence built for restaurant operators. It sees everything happening in your competitive landscape and delivers it in the order that matters — during the window where you can still act.

Brand Values

Signal over noise

Not a dashboard. A briefing. We compress, not collect.

Speed with confidence

Intelligence arrives when it's actionable. Every finding carries a confidence score.

Operator-first language

We speak covers and competitive sets, not dashboards and data lakes.

Powered By

Ticket is powered by Vatic — Alive Labs' competitive intelligence engine. Vatic is the technology layer; Ticket is the product restaurant operators interact with. Ticket owns: marketing site, wordmark, color tokens, mark, and positioning voice. Ticket inherits from Vatic: the entire product UI, interaction patterns, terminology, data visualization, dark mode, accessibility standards, and email architecture.

02 · Mark
The Tear-Off T

The mark is a T with a single perforation at the crossbar-stem junction. The crossbar is the machine — the printer, the POS, the source. The stem is the ticket printing out below it. The perforation sits right where you'd tear. The ticket is still attached, still coming. The bottom edge just ends — the receipt is still printing.

03 · Wordmark
Barlow Condensed 600

TICKET

Specs

Font: Barlow Condensed, weight 600. Case: all uppercase. Tracking: tight (-0.01em). The condensed form is the brand's compression made visible — narrow letterforms, no wasted space.

Rules

Always uppercase. Always Barlow Condensed 600. Never at the same size as an Instrument Serif headline. When adjacent to the mark, the mark provides visual separation — the wordmark never sits directly next to an Instrument Serif headline without a buffer.

05 · Typography
Three Layers, Three Jobs

Compression Layer

Barlow Condensed

Wordmark, nav, labels, section headers. Weights: 500 + 600 only.

Intelligence Layer

Instrument Serif

Display headlines, hero text, stat callouts. Regular + italic. Min 1.2rem.

Clarity Layer

Inter

Body text, data labels, UI copy. Shared with Vatic and Neat.

Type Rules

1. Instrument Serif minimum: 1.2rem / 19px on screen. Never smaller.

2. Instrument Serif italic: editorial emphasis only — accent phrases, pull quotes. Never in nav, labels, or buttons.

3. Instrument Serif headlines: aim for <60 characters. Wide set-width makes long lines unwieldy.

4. Barlow Condensed weights: 500 (labels, nav, metadata) and 600 (section headers, wordmark, buttons). No 400 or 700 in production.

5. Never set Barlow Condensed and Instrument Serif at the same size. They occupy distinct size bands.

6. Wordmark ↔ headline proximity: always separate with the mark, a spatial gap, or a color shift.

Google Fonts

Barlow Condensed: weights 500, 600. Instrument Serif: 400, 400 italic. Inter: 300–700 (variable).

06 · Brand Voice
The Expeditor

The Expeditor is the person at the pass who sees every ticket, orchestrates timing between stations, and ensures nothing leaves the kitchen wrong. They're the calmest person in the room because they're the most informed. When they speak, you listen — not because they're loud, but because they're right.

Direct

Lead with the finding. No throat-clearing, no context-setting. The operator needs the signal, not the methodology.

Not: Blunt or dismissive
Urgent

Speaks in the tempo of service — purposeful, time-aware, forward-moving. Always anchored to when it matters.

Not: Alarmist or breathless
Grounded

We speak the operator's language. Covers, pricing, the competitive set, the market radius. Not data science, not marketing.

Not: Performative or over-themed

Vocabulary Guide

Ticket references the operator's world to show fluency, not to theme every sentence. The line: would an actual operator say this to another operator? If not, it's performance.

Severity-to-Tone Mapping

Three tiers that map signal severity to the tone spectrum. Use these to systematically apply the right voice temperature to any notification, alert, or briefing section.

High

Competitor pricing change >10%. New opening in radius. Location closure. Menu overhaul detected.

Tone: High Alert. Short sentences. Numbers first. No hedging. The expeditor calling a fire.

Moderate

Pricing change <10%. New social campaign. Review pattern shift. Delivery/hours change.

Tone: Briefing. Structured, complete, still concise. The pre-service rundown.

Informational

Minor review volume change. Social post frequency shift. No-overlap new opening. Market summary.

Tone: Insight. More nuance, cooler tempo. Worth watching, not worth worrying about yet.

Closing Rule

Every Ticket story ends with the operator knowing first.

Content Principles

Signal first, story second

Lead with the number, the change, the finding. Context comes after.

Confidence is earned, not declared

Show a price change with a timestamp and a confidence score. The data carries the authority.

Time-stamp everything

"Prices changed" is noise. "Prices changed this morning" is intelligence.

Scale tone to signal

A competitor closing is urgent. A minor review shift is not. The voice matches the signal.

08 · Visual Direction
Thermal, Not Cozy

The marketing site is the window; the tool is the kitchen. Same brand, different energy. The site gets to be stark, confident, data-forward. The tool gets to be focused, clean, functional.

Photography

Operator-world close-ups — a phone screen with competitor data, a receipt on a counter, a manager before the doors open. Tight framing that says "we know your world" without faking a kitchen scene. AI-generated is acceptable for detail shots. No wide shots, no lifestyle photography, no plated food.

Supporting · Atmospheric

Texture

Print-world materials as ambient background layer — thermal paper grain, receipt edges, dot-matrix patterns, warm printer haze. These live as subtle overlays and section textures, not as illustrations. They create warmth and utility without narrating a metaphor.

Ambient · Background

Data Compositions

Stat-forward hero sections: a price change with a confidence score, a competitive set comparison, a market radius with activity counts. Rendered in the Ticket palette on dark surfaces. Communicates "your market, compressed" as a hero-level visual. Not a screenshot — an art-directed data moment.

Hero-level · Distinctive

Product Wireframes

Illustrative wireframes in the brand palette showing what the tool does — alerts, briefings, competitive set views — without committing to specific UI. Same approach as the Vatic and Alive Labs sites. No literal screenshots until the tool is publicly launched and visually locked.

Narrative · Illustrative

No lifestyle food photography

No smiling chefs, no plated dishes, no dining rooms. This is an intelligence tool for operators, not a consumer food app.

No kitchen/BOH imagery

The kitchen line is the operator's stress, not their aspiration. Never show cooking-show imagery. The vocabulary is front-of-house intelligence, not back-of-house operations.

No decorative gradients or soft UI

No soft shadows, no rounded-friendly aesthetic, no data-viz overlaid on photos. Keep data visualization as its own artifact. Sharp contrast, dark dominant.

09 · Page Structure
The Scroll Story

Single page, dark-dominant, story-driven scroll. No light/dark toggle on the marketing site. The page reads like a briefing — each section delivers one signal, in order of importance.

1

The Opening

Dark. Ink. The mark, the tagline, and a data composition hero — a price change with confidence score, a competitive set count. The brand introduces itself with a signal, not a sentence.

Dark · Urgent
2

The Problem

Warm. Thermal or Newsprint. What it's like to run a restaurant without competitive intelligence — you're the last to know when the place across the street changes pricing. Operator-world photography as texture.

3

What Ticket Does

Core value prop. Narrative wireframes showing the alert flow, competitive set view, briefing format. Each wireframe tells a micro-story. Framed in Ink containers against Newsprint.

4

The Market View

The data composition gets its own moment. A stylized competitive set map or market radius visualization. "Your market. Every competitor. Continuously monitored."

Dramatic · Data-Forward

How It Works

Brief, scannable. Three or four steps. Simple, clear. A visual exhale — quieter, more functional.

Quiet · Functional

Back to dark. Ink. CTA. The mark. "Read the ticket." The page ends the way it started — definitive.

Dark · Definitive

11 · Implementation
Vatic Theme Override

Ticket's visual identity is applied via a CSS theme file that overrides Vatic's default tokens. Apply [data-brand="ticket"] to <html> or <body> and import ticket-vatic-theme.css after globals.css.

Setup in layout.tsx:

import { Barlow_Condensed, Instrument_Serif } from 'next/font/google'; const barlowCondensed = Barlow_Condensed({ subsets: ['latin'], weight: ['500', '600'], variable: '--font-barlow-condensed', }); const instrumentSerif = Instrument_Serif({ subsets: ['latin'], weight: ['400'], style: ['normal', 'italic'], variable: '--font-instrument-serif', }); // Add both .variable to body className // Add data-brand="ticket" to html or body

Integration Checklist
1.
Load fonts via next/font/google in layout.tsx — Barlow_Condensed with variable: '--font-barlow-condensed' and Instrument_Serif with variable: '--font-instrument-serif'. Add both variable classes to the html element.
2.
Import the theme — add @import "ticket-vatic-theme.css" after globals.css. Selector specificity ensures overrides work without !important.
3.
Set the attribute — render data-brand="ticket" on the html element conditionally (subdomain, org type, or feature flag). Theme activates instantly; remove attribute to revert.
4.
Verify — check both light and dark modes. Landing page overrides (gradients, glass panels, ambient effects) are included. Scrollbar and selection colors adapt automatically.
