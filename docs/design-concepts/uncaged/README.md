# Ticket design concepts — UN-CAGED round (2026-06-26)

The redo after the diagnosis (`docs/design-gap-diagnosis-2026-06-26.md`). Brief: **ignore all Ticket brand
except color** (rust `#B85C38` signature + slate/gold/teal/ink; free on grounds), **push wild, dashboard-rich,
premium, mobile/tablet-first**, **represent the competitive intelligence visually** (steal-the-cue vs
press-the-advantage), ticket-object motif optional. Built from a 16-agent panel (8 worlds → adversarial
critique → 4 selected, none timid → realized as responsive HTML). Supersedes the timid editorial v1 in the
parent folder. **Concepts for review — not shipped.** Open the `.html` files directly (self-contained,
responsive; resize to phone width to see mobile).

## The four
| File | Tier | What it is |
|---|---|---|
| `refined-bold-the-pass.html` | refined-bold | **The Pass** — dark, restrained-but-rich production spine. One unmistakable anchor per insight, a longitudinal "your position vs the set" chart as a second hero beat, a racing head-to-head bar. The safest to ship; still nothing like v1. |
| `bold-the-expo-rail.html` | bold | **The Expo Rail** — the ticket/chit metaphor made fully load-bearing + kinetic. The home *is* the rail; plays "print in"; the core verb is BUMP/CLEAR. Most product-native. |
| `bold-the-feed-line.html` | bold | **The Feed Line** — an **asymmetric** card system: THEIR world is imagery to study (photo-forward), YOUR world is metrics to lean into (data-forward). The sharpest structural take on steal-cue vs press-advantage. |
| `experimental-the-line.html` | experimental | **The Line — Contested Territory** — your block as contested territory: every competitor move lights up as heat, every win you bank cools into your color. The most ambitious data-representation swing. |

Shared spine (all four): dark, warm-not-cold ground; **rust = the through-line / "you"**, teal = you're
winning, alert/red = they're winning; one anchor per insight; real competitive content (stadium rush,
burnt-chicken convergence, "Reels 4x", $19 spirit night, "$5 Modelo boots"); meaningful data-viz (rings,
sparklines, gradient area charts, head-to-head bars, week calendar) + motion; explicit mobile mockups.

UX/functionality gaps the panel surfaced are logged in `docs/ux-gaps-tracker.md` (social-handle/competitor
management is the big one). Next: Bryan picks a direction (or a fusion) → we reel it in + apply to the real app.
